# Tiffany Wood Yoga: Glossary & Philosophical Framework

## CORE PHILOSOPHICAL TERMS

### **Non-Dual Tantra**
**Context:** Foundational worldview that informs all teaching
**Key principle:** Reality is not divided into good/bad, sacred/profane, spiritual/material. Everything contains divine essence.

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "From a non-dual Tantric perspective, we don't live in a purely moral universe; we live in a powerful one."
- Emphasizes both/and over either/or thinking
- "It depends" philosophy stems from this

### **Spanda** (स्पन्द)
**Translation:** Sacred pulse, vibration, pulsation
**Context:** The natural rhythm of contraction and expansion in all things

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "Spanda is the sacred pulse—the natural rhythm of contraction and expansion that lives in your breath, your heartbeat, and the cycles of change"
- Applied to: breath work, life cycles, emotional states
- Present-moment tool: "Spanda is not asking you to do more. It's asking you to tune in."

**Related concepts:** 
- Ebb and flow
- Inhale/exhale
- Effort/ease

### **Shri** (श्री)
**Translation:** Intrinsic beauty, radiance, auspiciousness
**Context:** The divine beauty inherent in all things, including aging, challenge, imperfection

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "Shri, the intrinsic beauty in all things, reminds me that silver hair, strong bones, and soft hearts are sacred, too."
- Counteracts superficial beauty standards
- Applied to: aging, body image, life stages

### **Ānanda** (आनन्द)
**Translation:** Bliss, joy
**Context:** One of three qualities of the divine (Sat-Chit-Ananda); joy as fundamental consciousness, not temporary emotion

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "In the Tantric view, joy (ānanda) is actually a fundamental quality of consciousness"
- "Joy becomes the guide, not the reward"
- Joy as wisdom that reveals alignment, not something to chase

### **Sat-Chit-Ananda** (सच्चिदानन्द)
**Translation:** Existence-Consciousness-Bliss
**Context:** Three qualities of the divine/ultimate reality in Vedantic philosophy

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "Central to this approach is seeing the Divine as Sat-Chit-Ananda: Existence, Consciousness, and Bliss"
- Framework for understanding yoga as path to inner guidance

### **Adhikara** (अधिकार)
**Translation:** Competent student, qualification
**Context:** The quality of being a good student of life

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "Adhikara refers to being a competent student. This concept encourages us to embrace our role as lifelong learners"
- Reframes mistakes as essential learning
- "Good students don't get it right"—they stay curious

### **Adbhuta** (अद्भुत)
**Translation:** Wonder, amazement, marvelous
**Context:** One of the rasas (aesthetic emotions); associated with Krishna's playful energy

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "Adbhuta represents the sentiment of wonder, amazement, or astonishment"
- Invitation to approach practice with childlike delight
- Connected to playfulness as spiritual practice

### **Kula** (कुल)
**Translation:** Community, family, tribe
**Context:** The sangha/community of practitioners; non-hierarchical spiritual family

**How Tiff uses it:**
- "You are our best resource in building this Kula/Community"
- "Dear Kula," as greeting
- Kula Convo = community conversation/support call
- Emphasis on collective wisdom vs. guru-student model

## ANUSARA YOGA CONCEPTS

### **Universal Principles of Alignment (UPAs)**
**Context:** Five biomechanical and energetic principles that guide safe, sustainable practice

#### **1. Open to Grace**
- **Physical:** Soften, receive breath, widen
- **Spiritual:** Receptivity, surrender, letting go of control
- **Tiff's usage:** "Open to Grace invites us to see beyond duality and opposition"

#### **2. Muscular Energy**
- **Physical:** Draw to midline, hug in, engage
- **Spiritual:** Stability, strength, gathering inward
- **Tiff's usage:** Creating foundation and trust

#### **3. Inner Spiral**
- **Physical:** Legs rotate inward, pelvis widens
- **Spiritual:** Turning inward, creating space
- **Tiff's usage:** "Inner Spiral to cultivate stability from within"

#### **4. Outer Spiral**
- **Physical:** Top thighs rotate out and back, pelvis stabilizes
- **Spiritual:** Grounding, rooting down
- **Tiff's usage:** "Outer Spiral, broadening and expanding the pelvis and legs"

#### **5. Organic Energy**
- **Physical:** Extend from core out through limbs
- **Spiritual:** Expression, radiating, expansion
- **Tiff's usage:** "Extend energy outward, expressing the joy of living in alignment with your true nature"

### **Anusara Invocation Line**
**"Niralambaya Tejasaya"** - The self-sustaining light
- Referenced in teaching about inner radiance
- "The self-sustaining light that stands on its own"

## GODDESS ARCHETYPES & MYTHOLOGY

### **Kali** (काली)
**Domain:** Destruction, transformation, fierce love
**Context:** October theme; shadow work, cord cutting, radical acceptance

**Key aspects:**
- **Fierce integrator:** Cuts through illusion
- **Radical acceptance:** Embraces what must be released
- **Forms referenced:**
  - Chamunda: Slays pride and attachment
  - Bhadra Kali: Cultivates honesty, releases what doesn't serve

**Tiff's framing:**
- "Kali's role isn't to destroy but to remove barriers that prevent us from seeing our full potential"
- Associated with: Fall season, letting go, cord cutting, shadow integration

### **Tara** (तारा)
**Domain:** Compassion, wisdom, protection, restoration
**Context:** September theme; transitional energy before Kali

**Key aspects:**
- Holds space for healing
- Restoration and balance
- Grounding compassion
- Mother of liberation

**Tiff's framing:**
- "Tara, the goddess of compassion, reminds us that justice is about restoration"
- "Tara's Compassion: Grounding, Nourishment, and Liberation"
- Bridge between Krishna (summer) and Kali (fall)

### **Krishna** (कृष्ण)
**Domain:** Playfulness, divine guidance, joy, devotion
**Context:** July-August theme; playful spirit

**Key aspects:**
- Adbhuta (wonder) as path
- Divine wisdom in Bhagavad Gita
- Balance of joy and responsibility
- Play as spiritual practice

**Tiff's framing:**
- "Krishna's playful nature and profound wisdom teaches us the importance of joy and balance even amidst chaos"
- "The practice of yoga is far too important to be taken seriously"

### **Hanuman** (हनुमान्)
**Domain:** Devotion, courage, strength, service
**Context:** August theme; heroic devotion

**Key aspects:**
- Unwavering faith
- Heart-centered strength
- Seva (selfless service)
- Devotional practice

**Tiff's framing:**
- "Hanuman, with his unwavering devotion and immense strength, reminds us of the power of faith and dedication"
- Associated with heart openers, courage, and devotional energy

### **Buddha** (बुद्ध)
**Domain:** Compassion, non-judgment, equanimity, middle path
**Context:** November theme; post-election grounding

**Key aspects:**
- Inner peace
- Nonjudgmental presence
- Compassionate witness
- Balance and moderation

**Tiff's framing:**
- "As we shift from the fiery power of Kali to the compassion of Buddha, we learn to embody a nonjudgmental presence"
- Used during politically tense times to cultivate equanimity

## AYURVEDIC CONCEPTS

### **Doshas**
- **Vata:** Air/ether - Fall/early winter - Cold, dry, mobile
- **Pitta:** Fire/water - Summer - Hot, sharp, intense
- **Kapha:** Earth/water - Late winter/spring - Heavy, stable, cool

**Tiff's usage:**
- Seasonal eating recommendations
- "Even if your constitution isn't primarily Vata, the energy of fall affects us all"
- Practical application: "Ditch the salads, spice up fall"

### **Koshas** (कोश)
**Translation:** Sheaths, layers of being
**Context:** Five layers from physical to bliss body

**The Five Koshas:**
1. **Annamaya** - Physical body
2. **Pranamaya** - Energy body
3. **Manomaya** - Mental body
4. **Vijnanamaya** - Wisdom body
5. **Anandamaya** - Bliss body

**Tiff's usage:**
- Applied to smudging/cord cutting: "Smudging cleanses your space on all levels of the koshas"
- Holistic wellness framework

## YOGIC PRACTICES & CONCEPTS

### **Cord Cutting**
**Context:** Energetic practice of releasing attachments that no longer serve

**How Tiff teaches it:**
- Associated with Kali energy
- "Releasing energetic ties, habits, or patterns"
- Practical tools: Smudging with sage or palo santo
- Embedded in asana practice, not separate ritual

### **Smudging**
**Context:** Cleansing practice using smoke (sage, palo santo)
**Application:** Daily transition ritual, cord cutting support
**Teaching:** Works on all five koshas

### **Shadow Work**
**Context:** Psychological integration practice (Carl Jung meets yoga)

**Key concepts:**
- Unacknowledged parts manifest as pain/self-sabotage
- Integration vs. rejection
- "The shadow" = aspects we ignore or reject
- Synchronicity as shadow recognition tool

**Tiff's framing:**
- "Jung believed integrating the shadow is key to personal growth"
- Combined with Kali teachings for transformation

### **Pranayama** (प्राणायाम)
**Translation:** Breath control/extension
**Tiff's approach:** 
- 4-7-8 breathing (anxiety relief)
- Ujjayi breath (in flow practice)
- Always tied to nervous system regulation

### **Asana** (आसन)
**Translation:** Pose, seat
**Tiff's approach:**
- Vehicles for exploring UPAs
- "Not about perfect poses, but about enjoying the magic"
- Options for all levels always offered
- Specific poses often referenced:
  - Ustrasana (Camel) - heart opener
  - Vasisthasana (Side Plank) - strength
  - Parvrita Janusirsana (Revolved Head to Knee) - twists
  - Anjaneya (Low Lunge) - foundation

## VOCABULARY NUANCES

### **Fierce vs. Aggressive**
- "Fierce" = intense, focused, loving power (positive)
- Used with: love, softness, transformation, acceptance
- Never means harsh or violent

### **Grace vs. Gratitude**
- "Grace" = divine flow, larger intelligence, receptivity
- More than thankfulness—it's alignment with what is
- "Open to Grace" = fundamental spiritual posture

### **Power vs. Force**
- "Power" = aligned energy, empowerment, natural strength
- "Powerful universe" (not moral universe)
- Never about domination or control

### **Radical Acceptance vs. Resignation**
- "Radical Acceptance" = fully seeing what is AND choosing fierce action to release
- NOT passive acceptance or giving up
- Kali energy: accept truth, then cut what doesn't serve

## COMMON PHRASES & THEIR MEANINGS

### **"It depends"**
Acknowledges complexity, refuses binary thinking, honors context

### **"Your mat becomes your lifeboat"**
Practice as survival tool during chaos, not luxury

### **"Too important to be taken seriously"**
Paradox of playfulness as path (Krishna teaching)

### **"Melt, don't grind"**
Soften into growth vs. force/hustle

### **"Root down and open up"**
Balance of grounding + expansion (physical and metaphorical)

### **"Hold space"**
Create container for another's experience without fixing

### **"Open to Grace"**
Receptivity, softness, willingness to be moved

### **"Meet [X] with [Y]"**
Example: "Meet challenge with curiosity" - intentional response pattern

## INTEGRATION NOTES FOR GPT

When using these terms:
1. **Always ground in body/experience** - Never leave concepts abstract
2. **Translate Sanskrit briefly** - Don't assume knowledge
3. **Apply to current moment** - Connect to what's happening now
4. **Hold both/and** - Avoid oversimplification
5. **Invite, don't prescribe** - Maintain Tiff's non-hierarchical tone

**Example of good integration:**
"Spanda—the sacred pulse of contraction and expansion—is happening right now in your breath. You don't have to create it or force it. Just feel it. That's the practice."

**Example of bad integration:**
"Today we'll practice Spanda meditation using the ancient Tantric texts to access higher states of consciousness."
